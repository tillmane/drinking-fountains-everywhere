Fountains For All is a community-built map of public drinking water fountains — because staying hydrated in your city shouldn't require a credit card or a plastic bottle. Our goal: clean water within 300 meters, wherever you are.

Access to clean drinking water is a basic human right.

A Little Background

As an avid urban runner I was quick to learn that if I wanted to run more than a few miles in my city, I wanted to know where the water is. But access to clean drinking water in public space isn't just a runner's problem — it's a public health issue. We think everyone within 300 meters of wherever they are should be able to find a fountain. That's the goal.

How It Works

View the map. Browse over 500 public fountains across Seattle and Bellevue, sourced from city GIS and OpenStreetMap data.

Find what you need. Filter by location, check whether a fountain is working, and see fountain features such as dog bowl or bottle filler.

Help keep it accurate. Rate fountains with a 👍 or 👎 based on your experience. Can't find one that's on the map? Report it. There but shut off for winter? Report that too.

Mostly thumbs up = good water here.
Mostly thumbs down = skip it.
